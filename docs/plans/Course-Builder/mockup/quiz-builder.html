Q1 Ribeye Knowledge

How many days is the Prime Dry-Aged Ribeye aged at minimum before serving?

Explanation

Our Prime Dry-Aged Ribeye is aged for a minimum of 28 days in-house. This duration allows for optimal moisture loss (concentrating flavor) and enzymatic tenderization without over-aging the cut.

Q2 Allergen Awareness
Active Shown 10x · 60% correct

Which of the following allergens are present in the Ribeye's herb butter and dry-rub? Select the most complete answer.

Explanation

The herb butter contains dairy, and the dry-rub includes mustard powder. Always confirm both allergens with guests. For dairy-free service, substitute olive oil and sea salt. There is no gluten, soy, or shellfish in this dish.

Q3 Wine Pairing
Active Shown 8x · 75% correct

What is the recommended primary wine pairing for the Dry-Aged Ribeye?

Explanation

The Caymus Cabernet Sauvignon's bold tannins cut through the rich fat of the dry-aged ribeye, while its dark fruit notes complement the char from our 1200°F broiler. The backup recommendation is the Stag's Leap Artemis for guests who prefer a softer profile.
