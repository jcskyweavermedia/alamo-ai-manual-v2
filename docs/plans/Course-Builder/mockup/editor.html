Additional Callout Variants
Caution: Resting Time
Never rush a ribeye to the table. It must rest 3-5 minutes after coming off the broiler. Cutting into it immediately releases all the juices and results in a dry steak. If a guest complains about wait time, explain that resting is part of the cooking process.
Did You Know?
Dry-aging reduces the weight of a steak by 15-20% through moisture evaporation. That's why our dry-aged cuts cost more — we're paying for beef that literally shrinks. This is a great talking point when guests ask about the price premium.
Key Point: Wine Pairing
The ribeye pairs best with full-bodied reds. Our top recommendation is the Caymus Cabernet Sauvignon — the bold tannins cut through the fat and complement the char. Second choice: the Stag's Leap Artemis for a softer, more approachable pairing.
