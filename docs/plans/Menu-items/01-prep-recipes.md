## PR-1: House Steak Seasoning

**Type:** Dry blend | **Yield:** ~1 cup | **Shelf Life:** 3 months (airtight, room temp)

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Kosher salt (Diamond Crystal) | 1/2 cup |
| Coarse black pepper | 1/4 cup |
| Garlic powder | 2 tbsp |
| Onion powder | 2 tbsp |
| Smoked paprika | 1 tbsp |
| Dried thyme | 1 tbsp |
| Cayenne pepper | 1 tsp |

### Method

1. Combine all ingredients in a large mixing bowl.
2. Whisk thoroughly until evenly distributed — no clumps of cayenne.
3. Transfer to a labeled airtight container or large shaker.
4. Store at room temperature, away from heat and moisture.

### Storage

- Airtight container at room temperature.
- Label with date. Discard after 3 months (spices lose potency).

### Notes

- Used on **every steak** and the **Steak Bites** appetizer. Mix in bulk.
- Diamond Crystal kosher salt is critical — if substituting Morton's, use half the amount (Morton's is denser).
- Adjust cayenne to house preference. Default is mild warmth, not heat-forward.
- Do NOT use fine table salt — kosher salt gives better adhesion and even coverage on thick cuts.

## PR-2: Herb Compound Butter

**Type:** Compound butter | **Yield:** 2 logs (~24 medallions) | **Shelf Life:** 2 weeks refrigerated, 3 months frozen

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Unsalted butter, softened to room temp | 1 lb (4 sticks) |
| Garlic, roasted and mashed (see step 1) | All cloves from 2 whole heads (~20-24 cloves) |
| Fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely minced | 3 tbsp |
| Fresh thyme leaves, finely minced | 1 tbsp |
| Fresh chives, finely minced | 2 tbsp |
| Lemon zest (microplaned) | 1 tsp |
| Kosher salt | 1 tsp |
| Black pepper, freshly ground | 1/2 tsp |

### Method

1. **Roast garlic:** Slice the top 1/4 off 2 whole garlic heads to expose cloves. Place cut-side up on a sheet of foil, drizzle each with 1 tsp olive oil, wrap tightly. Roast at 400°F for 40 minutes until cloves are golden and soft. Cool completely. Squeeze cloves out of skins, mash to a paste with the flat side of a knife.
2. **Combine:** In a stand mixer with paddle attachment (or a large bowl with a spatula), beat softened butter until smooth and creamy, about 1 minute.
3. Add mashed roasted garlic, parsley, thyme, chives, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. Beat on low until evenly incorporated, scraping sides as needed. Do not whip — you want it blended, not airy.
4. **Roll into logs:** Lay a 14-inch sheet of plastic wrap on the counter. Spoon half the butter in a rough line along the center. Fold plastic over and roll tightly into a uniform log, ~1.5 inches in diameter. Twist both ends tightly to seal. Repeat with remaining butter.
5. **Chill:** Refrigerate logs at least 2 hours until firm.
6. **Portion:** Slice into 1/2-inch medallions to order. Each log yields ~12 medallions.

### Storage

- **Refrigerated:** Wrapped in plastic, up to 2 weeks.
- **Frozen:** Wrap logs in plastic, then foil. Up to 3 months. Thaw in fridge overnight before slicing.

### Notes

- This is the **highest-traffic prep** in the kitchen — it goes on all 5 steaks, the Truffle Mashed Potatoes, and the Steak Bites.
- Roast garlic in bulk (6+ heads). Extra mashed garlic stores 1 week refrigerated in a covered container with a thin film of olive oil on top.
- Butter MUST be room temperature before mixing. Cold butter will not incorporate herbs evenly.
- Keep 1 log in the fridge (active) and 1 in the freezer (backup). Pull frozen log to fridge the night before needed.

## PR-3: Truffle Mashed Potatoes

**Type:** Hot side (batch) | **Yield:** ~12 portions (6 oz each) | **Shelf Life:** 2 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered | 5 lbs |
| Heavy cream, warmed | 1 cup |
| Herb Compound Butter (PR-2), sliced | 4 medallions (~4 oz) |
| Truffle oil (black truffle, quality brand) | 2 tbsp |
| Kosher salt | 1 tbsp + more to taste |
| White pepper | 1/2 tsp |

### Method

1. Place peeled, quartered potatoes in a large pot. Cover with cold water by 2 inches. Add 1 tbsp salt.
2. Bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce to a steady simmer. Cook 18-22 minutes until fork-tender — a knife should slide through with zero resistance.
3. Drain thoroughly in a colander. Return potatoes to the hot, empty pot. Set over low heat for 1-2 minutes, shaking the pot, to evaporate excess surface moisture.
4. Pass potatoes through a ricer or food mill back into the pot. **Do NOT use a food processor or immersion blender** — overworking releases starch and creates gluey texture.
5. Set pot over low heat. Fold in warm cream and **Herb Compound Butter (PR-2)** medallions. Stir gently with a spatula until butter is melted and fully incorporated.
6. Fold in truffle oil. Season with salt and white pepper to taste.
7. Transfer to a half hotel pan. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface (prevents skin from forming). Hold in steam table at 140-150°F, or cover and hold in a low oven (170°F).

### Storage & Reheating

- **Holding (same-day):** Steam table at 140-150°F, up to 4 hours. Stir occasionally.
- **Refrigerated:** Cool, cover, refrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat on low heat with a splash of warm cream, stirring frequently, until 165°F.

### Notes

- Uses **Herb Compound Butter (PR-2)** — the roasted garlic and herbs in the butter provide the savory depth. No additional garlic needed.
- Yukon Golds are non-negotiable — their waxy-starchy balance and golden color define this dish. Do not substitute russets (too starchy/dry) or red potatoes (too waxy).
- Use quality truffle oil with real truffle pieces (check ingredient label). Synthetic truffle oils (2,4-dithiapentane) taste harsh.
- White pepper is used instead of black to keep the mash clean-looking (no black specks).

## PR-4: Creamed Spinach

**Type:** Hot side (batch) | **Yield:** ~7 portions (5 oz each) | **Shelf Life:** 2 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh baby spinach | 2 lbs (about 4 large bags) |
| Unsalted butter | 3 tbsp |
| Shallots, finely diced | 2 medium (~1/4 cup) |
| Garlic cloves, minced | 4 |
| All-purpose flour | 2 tbsp |
| Heavy cream | 1.5 cups |
| Whole milk | 1/2 cup |
| Freshly grated nutmeg | 1/4 tsp |
| Parmesan cheese (Parmigiano-Reggiano), finely grated | 1/4 cup |
| Kosher salt | 1 tsp + more to taste |
| White pepper | 1/4 tsp |

### Method

1. **Blanch spinach:** Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil. Add all spinach, pushing it down with tongs. Blanch exactly 60 seconds — no more.
2. **Shock:** Immediately drain and transfer spinach to a large ice bath. Let cool completely (2-3 minutes).
3. **Squeeze dry:** Pull spinach from ice bath in handfuls. Wrap in a clean kitchen towel (or several layers of cheesecloth) and wring out as hard as possible. You want the spinach bone-dry — wet spinach is the #1 cause of watery creamed spinach. Roughly chop the squeezed spinach.
4. **Make cream sauce:** In a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium heat, melt butter. Add shallots, cook 2 minutes until soft and translucent. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Sprinkle flour over the shallot-garlic mixture. Stir constantly for 1 minute to cook the raw flour taste out (blond roux). Do not brown.
6. Slowly pour in cream and milk while whisking continuously. Bring to a gentle simmer. Whisk until sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 3-4 minutes.
7. Stir in nutmeg, Parmesan, salt, and white pepper.
8. **Fold in spinach:** Add the chopped blanched spinach to the cream sauce. Stir to coat evenly. Taste and adjust salt.
9. Transfer to a half hotel pan. Hold in steam table at 140-150°F or low oven (170°F).

### Storage & Reheating

- **Holding (same-day):** Steam table at 140-150°F, up to 4 hours.
- **Refrigerated:** Cool, cover, refrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat on low heat, stirring frequently. Add a splash of cream if it has thickened too much.

### Notes

- **Squeezing the spinach dry is the most critical step.** If you skip this, excess water will leach into the cream sauce during holding and you'll have a soupy mess by the second hour of service.
- The roux (flour + butter) provides body and prevents the sauce from breaking during long holds. Do not skip it.
- Nutmeg must be freshly grated on a microplane — pre-ground nutmeg from a jar is flat and stale.
- 2 lbs fresh spinach looks like a mountain but cooks down to almost nothing — that's normal.

## PR-5: Cocktail Sauce

**Type:** Cold sauce | **Yield:** ~2 cups | **Shelf Life:** 7 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Ketchup (Heinz) | 1 cup |
| Prepared horseradish (not cream-style) | 3 tbsp (adjust to taste) |
| Fresh lemon juice | 2 tbsp (~1 lemon) |
| Worcestershire sauce | 1 tsp |
| Hot sauce (Tabasco original) | 1/2 tsp |
| Celery salt | 1/4 tsp |

### Method

1. Combine all ingredients in a mixing bowl. Whisk until smooth and uniform.
2. Taste. Adjust horseradish up to 1/4 cup for a spicier version.
3. Transfer to a labeled squeeze bottle or deli container with lid.
4. Refrigerate at least 4 hours before service. Overnight is ideal — the flavors need time to meld.

### Storage

- Labeled deli container or squeeze bottle, refrigerated. Up to 7 days.

### Notes

- **Heinz ketchup specifically.** Generic brands vary in sweetness and acidity, throwing off the balance.
- Use **prepared horseradish** (grated horseradish in vinegar), NOT horseradish cream sauce (which contains sour cream and is too mild).
- The horseradish punch fades after 3-4 days as the volatile compounds dissipate. For a busy service, make fresh every 3 days. For low volume, make smaller batches.
- This sauce is served in a ramekin alongside the shrimp cocktail.

## PR-6: Blue Cheese Dressing

**Type:** Cold sauce | **Yield:** ~3 cups | **Shelf Life:** 5 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Mayonnaise (Best Foods / Hellmann's) | 1/2 cup |
| Sour cream | 1/2 cup |
| Buttermilk | 1/2 cup |
| Blue cheese crumbles (quality: Maytag, Roquefort, or Gorgonzola) | 6 oz total, divided |
| Fresh lemon juice | 1 tbsp |
| Garlic, finely minced | 1 small clove |
| Worcestershire sauce | 1 tsp |
| Kosher salt | 1/2 tsp |
| Black pepper, freshly ground | 1/4 tsp |

### Method

1. In a large bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, sour cream, and buttermilk until smooth.
2. **Mash 4 oz** of the blue cheese in a separate small bowl with a fork, pressing into a rough paste. This creates the creamy base flavor.
3. Add the mashed cheese, lemon juice, garlic, Worcestershire, salt, and pepper to the buttermilk mixture. Whisk until combined.
4. **Gently fold in the remaining 2 oz** of whole blue cheese crumbles with a spatula. These stay intact for the classic chunky texture.
5. Transfer to a labeled squeeze bottle or deli container.
6. Refrigerate at least 4 hours before service. Overnight is ideal.

### Storage

- Labeled container, refrigerated. Up to 5 days.
- If dressing thickens after a day or two, stir in a small splash of buttermilk to loosen.

### Notes

- **Two-texture method:** Mashing half the cheese into the base gives creaminess and uniform flavor. Folding in the other half whole gives the chunky bites guests expect. This is what separates house-made from bottled.
- Quality blue cheese makes or breaks this dressing. Maytag (Iowa) is the classic American steakhouse choice. Roquefort or Gorgonzola dolce also work well.
- This dressing is the only sauce on the Wedge Salad — it needs to be bold enough to stand on its own.

## PR-7: Berry Compote

**Type:** Dessert sauce | **Yield:** ~3 cups | **Shelf Life:** 5 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh strawberries, hulled and quartered | 2 cups (~12 oz) |
| Fresh blueberries | 1 cup (~6 oz) |
| Granulated sugar | 1/3 cup |
| Fresh lemon juice | 1 tbsp |
| Vanilla extract (pure) | 1 tsp |
| Water | 2 tbsp |

### Method

1. Combine strawberries, blueberries, sugar, lemon juice, and water in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
2. Stir occasionally as sugar dissolves and berries begin to release their juice (3-4 minutes).
3. Once bubbling, reduce heat to low. Simmer gently 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the liquid thickens to a loose, spoonable consistency. Some berries should break down while others remain mostly whole — this mix of textures is desirable.
4. Remove from heat. Stir in vanilla extract.
5. Cool to room temperature. Transfer to a labeled deli container.

### Storage & Reheating

- **Refrigerated:** Up to 5 days.
- **Serving temp:** Room temperature or gently warmed. To warm: microwave 20 seconds or heat in a small saucepan over low, stirring.

### Notes

- Do NOT cook until stiff or jam-like. The compote should be **loose and pourable** — it needs to cascade over the cheesecake slice and pool on the plate.
- This compote tops **both desserts** (Cheesecake and Brownie). Keep par levels sufficient for both.
- **Off-season substitute:** Frozen mixed berries work. Skip the 2 tbsp water (frozen berries release extra liquid). Add 1-2 minutes to the simmering time.
- The lemon juice brightens the flavor and prevents the compote from tasting flat or one-dimensionally sweet.

## PR-8: New York Cheesecake

**Type:** Dessert (full batch bake) | **Yield:** 1 cake (10-inch springform), 12 slices | **Shelf Life:** 5 days refrigerated

### Crust Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Graham crackers, finely crushed | 2 cups (~14 full sheets) |
| Unsalted butter, melted | 6 tbsp |
| Granulated sugar | 2 tbsp |
| Kosher salt | pinch |

### Filling Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cream cheese (full-fat, brick-style), room temp | 2 lbs (four 8-oz blocks) |
| Granulated sugar | 3/4 cup |
| Sour cream, room temp | 1/2 cup |
| Heavy cream | 1/4 cup |
| Pure vanilla extract | 2 tsp |
| Lemon zest (microplaned) | 1 tsp |
| All-purpose flour | 2 tbsp |
| Large eggs, room temp | 4 |

### Method

**Crust:**
1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
2. In a bowl, combine crushed graham crackers, melted butter, sugar, and salt. Mix until it resembles wet sand.
3. Press mixture firmly into the bottom and 1 inch up the sides of a 10-inch springform pan. Use the flat bottom of a measuring cup to pack it evenly.
4. Bake 10 minutes until set. Remove and let cool while making filling.

**Filling:**
5. In a stand mixer with paddle attachment, beat cream cheese on medium speed until completely smooth, about 2 minutes. Scrape down the bowl and paddle frequently — lumps are your enemy.
6. Add sugar. Beat 2 minutes until fluffy and dissolved.
7. Add sour cream, heavy cream, vanilla, and lemon zest. Beat on medium-low until just combined. Scrape bowl.
8. Add flour. Mix on low just until incorporated.
9. Add eggs **one at a time**, mixing on the lowest speed after each, just until the yolk disappears. **Do NOT over-beat after adding eggs** — over-beating incorporates air, which causes cracking and a puffy-then-collapsed texture.
10. Pour filling over the cooled crust. Tap the pan gently on the counter 3 times to release air bubbles.

**Water Bath Bake:**
11. Wrap the outside of the springform pan tightly in **2 layers of heavy-duty aluminum foil** (this prevents water from seeping into the crust).
12. Place the wrapped springform inside a large roasting pan. Place in the oven. Carefully pour hot tap water into the roasting pan until it reaches halfway up the sides of the springform.
13. Bake at 325°F for 70-80 minutes. The cheesecake is done when the edges are set and the **center 3 inches jiggle slightly** when the pan is gently shaken (like Jello, not liquid). Start checking at 65 minutes — ovens vary.
14. **Cool slowly:** Turn the oven off. Crack the oven door open about 2 inches (prop with a wooden spoon). Let the cheesecake cool inside the oven for 1 full hour. This gradual cooling prevents cracking.
15. Remove from oven and water bath. Peel off foil. Run a thin knife around the inside edge of the springform (prevents the cake from pulling and cracking as it contracts). Refrigerate **uncovered** for at least 6 hours, ideally overnight.

**Pre-Cut for Service:**
16. Remove springform ring. Using a thin, sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry between each cut, score the cheesecake into 12 equal slices.
17. Keep sliced cake on its base, covered with plastic wrap, refrigerated until service.

### Storage

- **Refrigerated:** Covered, up to 5 days. Cheesecake actually improves in flavor after 1-2 days.
- **Frozen (whole, pre-sliced):** Wrap tightly in plastic then foil. Up to 1 month. Thaw overnight in fridge.

### Notes

- **All dairy must be room temperature.** Cold cream cheese = lumps that won't beat out. Pull everything from the fridge 2 hours ahead.
- The water bath provides gentle, even heat and steam — this is what gives NY cheesecake its signature dense, creamy texture without cracks.
- This is a DENSE, New York-style cheesecake — it should not be light or fluffy. If it puffs up significantly during baking, the eggs were over-beaten.
- Hot-water knife technique for cutting is essential. A cold, dry knife drags through the filling and makes ragged slices.

## PR-9: Brownie Batch

**Type:** Dessert (batch bake) | **Yield:** 1 pan (9x13), 12 portions | **Shelf Life:** 3 days at room temp (wrapped), 5 days refrigerated

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Dark chocolate (60-70% cacao), coarsely chopped | 8 oz |
| Unsalted butter | 1 cup (2 sticks) |
| Granulated sugar | 1.5 cups |
| Large eggs | 4 |
| Pure vanilla extract | 2 tsp |
| All-purpose flour | 1 cup |
| Dutch-process cocoa powder | 1/3 cup |
| Kosher salt | 1/2 tsp |

### Method

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving overhang on two sides for easy removal. Spray parchment with non-stick cooking spray.
2. **Melt chocolate and butter:** Combine chopped chocolate and butter in a large heatproof bowl set over a pot of barely simmering water (double boiler). Stir frequently until fully melted and smooth. Remove bowl from heat. Let cool 5 minutes — the mixture should be warm, not hot (hot mixture will scramble the eggs).
3. Whisk sugar into the chocolate-butter mixture until combined.
4. Add eggs one at a time, whisking vigorously after each until the batter is glossy and smooth.
5. Whisk in vanilla extract.
6. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, and salt.
7. **Fold** (do not whisk) the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a rubber spatula. Mix until just combined and no dry streaks remain. Do NOT over-mix — over-mixing develops gluten and makes tough, cakey brownies.
8. Pour batter into the prepared 9x13 pan. Spread evenly with an offset spatula.
9. Bake 22-26 minutes. Test with a toothpick at 22 minutes — it should come out with **moist crumbs clinging to it**, NOT clean. Clean = over-baked. Moist crumbs = fudgy.
10. Cool completely in the pan on a wire rack (at least 1 hour). Brownies firm up significantly as they cool.
11. Lift out using the parchment overhang. Cut into 12 equal portions (3 rows x 4 columns).
12. Store in an airtight container, layered with parchment between layers.

### Storage

- **Room temp:** Wrapped or in airtight container, up to 3 days.
- **Refrigerated:** Up to 5 days. Bring to room temp or warm to order before serving.
- **Frozen:** Individually wrap portions in plastic. Up to 1 month. Thaw at room temp 30 min or warm directly from frozen (add 30 seconds to warm time).

### Notes

- **Pull at moist crumbs, not clean.** This is the most common mistake. The brownies continue cooking (carry-over heat) as they cool in the pan. If the toothpick comes out clean, they're already over-baked.
- **Dutch-process cocoa** (alkalized) gives a deeper, richer chocolate flavor and darker color. Natural cocoa (like Hershey's standard) is more acidic and lighter in color.
- **60-70% dark chocolate** is the sweet spot. Below 60% is too sweet/milky. Above 70% is too bitter for most guests.
- The double-boiler melt is gentler than microwaving and prevents scorched chocolate.
- Brownies are **warmed to order** during service (see BOH Plate Spec). They do not need to come out of the oven right before plating.
