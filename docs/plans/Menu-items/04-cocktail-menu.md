# Alamo Prime Steakhouse — Cocktail Menu

> **10 classic cocktails** befitting a premium steakhouse. 3 bar prep recipes support the program.
> Most cocktails are built from shelf ingredients — no prep needed. A few use house-made syrups and mix for elevated quality.

---

## Bar Prep Recipes

---

### BP-1: House Simple Syrup

**Yield:** ~2 cups | **Shelf Life:** 2 weeks refrigerated

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Granulated sugar | 1 cup |
| Water | 1 cup |

**Method:**
1. Combine sugar and water in a small saucepan over medium heat.
2. Stir until sugar is completely dissolved. Do not boil — just dissolve.
3. Remove from heat. Cool to room temperature.
4. Transfer to a labeled squeeze bottle or pour spout bottle.
5. Refrigerate.

**Used in:** Old Fashioned, Whiskey Sour, Espresso Martini

**Notes:**
- 1:1 ratio (standard). Do not use rich syrup (2:1) — it over-sweetens at the same pour.
- If syrup turns cloudy or shows mold, discard and make a fresh batch.

---

### BP-2: House Sour Mix

**Yield:** ~3 cups | **Shelf Life:** 5 days refrigerated

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh lemon juice | 1 cup (~8 lemons) |
| Fresh lime juice | 1/2 cup (~6 limes) |
| House Simple Syrup (BP-1) | 1 cup |
| Water | 1/4 cup |

**Method:**
1. Juice lemons and limes. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve to remove pulp and seeds.
2. Combine strained juices, **BP-1: House Simple Syrup**, and water in a large measuring cup or bowl.
3. Whisk to combine. Taste — it should be tart-forward with balanced sweetness.
4. Transfer to a labeled pour spout bottle.
5. Refrigerate.

**Used in:** Whiskey Sour, Sidecar

**Notes:**
- **Fresh juice only.** Bottled lemon/lime juice tastes flat and artificial — guests can tell.
- Juice oxidizes quickly. Make fresh every 3-5 days max.
- The water softens the acidity slightly, making the mix more versatile across cocktails.

---

### BP-3: Honey-Ginger Syrup

**Yield:** ~1.5 cups | **Shelf Life:** 10 days refrigerated

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Honey | 1 cup |
| Water | 1/2 cup |
| Fresh ginger root, peeled and sliced thin | 3 oz (~3-inch piece) |

**Method:**
1. Combine honey, water, and sliced ginger in a small saucepan over medium heat.
2. Stir until honey dissolves. Bring to a gentle simmer.
3. Reduce heat to low. Simmer 10 minutes — the ginger infuses the syrup.
4. Remove from heat. Let steep an additional 15 minutes off heat.
5. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing ginger slices to extract flavor. Discard solids.
6. Cool to room temperature. Transfer to a labeled squeeze bottle.
7. Refrigerate.

**Used in:** Penicillin

**Notes:**
- This is the signature house syrup — the one unique element on the cocktail list that can't be replicated from a bottle.
- Honey variety matters. A mild clover or wildflower honey works best. Dark buckwheat honey is too aggressive.
- The ginger heat mellows over the 10-day shelf life. Fresh batches will be spicier.

## Cocktails

### 1. Old Fashioned

The quintessential steakhouse cocktail. Ordered more than any other drink at American steakhouses.

**Glass:** Rocks glass | **Method:** Built in glass

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Bourbon (Woodford Reserve or Bulleit) | 2 oz |
| House Simple Syrup (BP-1) | 1/4 oz |
| Angostura bitters | 3 dashes |
| Orange peel | 1 wide strip |
| Luxardo cherry | 1 |
| Large ice cube | 1 |

**Procedure:**
1. Add **BP-1: House Simple Syrup** and Angostura bitters to a rocks glass.
2. Add the large ice cube.
3. Pour bourbon over the ice.
4. Stir gently with a bar spoon, 10-12 rotations — you are chilling and diluting, not shaking.
5. Express an orange peel over the drink (hold peel over the glass, skin-side down, and squeeze to spray oils across the surface). Run the peel around the rim.
6. Drop the peel into the glass. Add a Luxardo cherry.
7. Serve.

### 2. Classic Martini

Timeless. Guests will specify gin or vodka, up or on the rocks, olives or twist.

**Glass:** Chilled martini glass | **Method:** Stirred

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gin (Tanqueray or Hendrick's) or Vodka (Grey Goose or Tito's) | 2.5 oz |
| Dry vermouth (Dolin or Noilly Prat) | 0.5 oz |
| Lemon twist or olives | garnish |

**Procedure:**
1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
2. Add gin (or vodka) and dry vermouth.
3. Stir 30 seconds — martinis are stirred, not shaken (stirring keeps it silky; shaking makes it cloudy).
4. Strain into a **chilled** martini glass (chill glasses in the freezer or with ice water).
5. Garnish with a lemon twist (expressed over the surface) or 2-3 cocktail olives on a pick.
6. Serve immediately.

**Variations guests will request:**
- "Dirty" — add 0.5 oz olive brine.
- "Extra dry" — reduce vermouth to a rinse (swirl and dump).
- "Wet" — increase vermouth to 0.75 oz.
- "Gibson" — garnish with cocktail onion instead of olive/twist.

### 3. Manhattan

The red-wine-lover's cocktail. Dark, rich, spirit-forward — perfect before or alongside a steak.

**Glass:** Chilled coupe | **Method:** Stirred

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Rye whiskey (Rittenhouse or Bulleit Rye) | 2 oz |
| Sweet vermouth (Carpano Antica Formula) | 1 oz |
| Angostura bitters | 2 dashes |
| Luxardo cherry | 1 |

**Procedure:**
1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
2. Add rye, sweet vermouth, and bitters.
3. Stir 30 seconds until well chilled.
4. Strain into a chilled coupe glass.
5. Garnish with a Luxardo cherry.
6. Serve.

### 4. Whiskey Sour

The most approachable whiskey cocktail on the list. Smooth, balanced, a gateway for non-whiskey drinkers.

**Glass:** Rocks glass | **Method:** Shaken

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Bourbon (Buffalo Trace or Maker's Mark) | 2 oz |
| House Sour Mix (BP-2) | 1.5 oz |
| Egg white (optional) | 1 |
| Angostura bitters | 2 dashes (garnish) |
| Orange wheel | garnish |

**Procedure:**
1. Add bourbon and **BP-2: House Sour Mix** to a shaker tin.
2. *(Optional — for a silky foam top)* Add 1 egg white. **Dry shake** (without ice) vigorously for 10 seconds to emulsify the egg white.
3. Add ice. Shake hard for 12-15 seconds.
4. Strain into a rocks glass over fresh ice.
5. Dash Angostura bitters on top of the foam (2-3 drops, drag a toothpick through for a design).
6. Garnish with an orange wheel on the rim.
7. Serve.

### 5. Negroni

Bitter, bold, and unapologetically Italian. A sophisticated aperitivo that pairs surprisingly well with steak.

**Glass:** Rocks glass | **Method:** Stirred

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gin (Beefeater or Tanqueray) | 1 oz |
| Campari | 1 oz |
| Sweet vermouth (Carpano Antica Formula) | 1 oz |
| Orange peel | 1 wide strip |

**Procedure:**
1. Add gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth to a rocks glass with a large ice cube.
2. Stir gently, 10-12 rotations.
3. Express an orange peel over the drink and drop it in.
4. Serve.

### 6. Espresso Martini

The most-ordered after-dinner cocktail in America right now. A dessert in a glass.

**Glass:** Chilled martini glass | **Method:** Shaken

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Vodka (Grey Goose or Ketel One) | 1.5 oz |
| Coffee liqueur (Kahlúa) | 0.75 oz |
| Fresh espresso, cooled | 1 oz |
| House Simple Syrup (BP-1) | 0.25 oz |
| Coffee beans | 3 (garnish) |

**Procedure:**
1. Pull a fresh shot of espresso. Let it cool 2-3 minutes (hot espresso will melt the ice too fast and make a watery drink).
2. Add vodka, Kahlúa, cooled espresso, and **BP-1: House Simple Syrup** to a shaker tin.
3. Add ice. **Shake hard** for 15 seconds — the vigorous shake creates the signature crema foam on top.
4. Double-strain (hawthorne + fine mesh) into a chilled martini glass.
5. Garnish with 3 coffee beans floated on the foam.
6. Serve immediately — the foam fades.

### 7. Margarita

Not traditional steakhouse, but universally popular. This is a proper margarita — no blender, no neon mix.

**Glass:** Rocks glass, half salt rim | **Method:** Shaken

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Tequila blanco (Patrón Silver or Casamigos) | 2 oz |
| Cointreau (or Grand Marnier for premium) | 1 oz |
| Fresh lime juice | 1 oz |
| Kosher salt | for rim |
| Lime wheel | garnish |

**Procedure:**
1. Run a lime wedge around half the rim of a rocks glass. Dip the wet half in kosher salt (half-rim lets guests choose salted or unsalted sips).
2. Add tequila, Cointreau, and fresh lime juice to a shaker tin with ice.
3. Shake hard for 12-15 seconds.
4. Strain into the prepared glass over fresh ice.
5. Garnish with a lime wheel.
6. Serve.

### 8. Sidecar

A classic that belongs on every steakhouse list. Cognac-based, citrusy, elegant — a sophisticated alternative to whiskey.

**Glass:** Chilled coupe, sugar rim | **Method:** Shaken

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cognac (Rémy Martin VSOP or Hennessy VS) | 2 oz |
| Cointreau | 0.75 oz |
| House Sour Mix (BP-2) | 0.75 oz |
| Granulated sugar | for rim |
| Orange twist | garnish |

**Procedure:**
1. Run an orange wedge around the rim of a chilled coupe. Dip in granulated sugar for a full sugar rim.
2. Add cognac, Cointreau, and **BP-2: House Sour Mix** to a shaker tin with ice.
3. Shake 12-15 seconds.
4. Double-strain into the prepared coupe.
5. Garnish with an expressed orange twist on the rim.
6. Serve.

### 9. Penicillin

A modern classic (invented 2005, already a staple). Smoky, sweet, spicy — the most interesting cocktail on the list.

**Glass:** Rocks glass | **Method:** Shaken

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Blended scotch (Monkey Shoulder or Dewar's) | 2 oz |
| Honey-Ginger Syrup (BP-3) | 0.75 oz |
| Fresh lemon juice | 0.75 oz |
| Islay scotch (Laphroaig 10) | 0.25 oz (float) |
| Candied ginger | 1 piece (garnish) |

**Procedure:**
1. Add blended scotch, **BP-3: Honey-Ginger Syrup**, and fresh lemon juice to a shaker tin with ice.
2. Shake hard for 12-15 seconds.
3. Strain into a rocks glass over a large ice cube.
4. **Float** the Islay scotch: slowly pour 0.25 oz over the back of a bar spoon so it sits on top of the drink (the smoky aroma hits the nose first).
5. Garnish with a piece of candied ginger on a cocktail pick.
6. Serve.
