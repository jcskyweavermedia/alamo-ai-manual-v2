# Alamo Prime Steakhouse — BOH Plate Specs

> **Line cook execution guide.** Each plate spec tells the cook exactly how to build and plate the dish during service.
> Prep recipes are referenced by their ID and exact name (e.g., **PR-2: Herb Compound Butter**).
> Items marked ★ are done at the start of each shift, not to order.

### APP-1: Classic Shrimp Cocktail

**Station:** Garde Manger (Cold)

#### Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Poach shrimp | Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Season with 2 tbsp kosher salt, 1 halved lemon, and 2 bay leaves. Reduce heat until water is at a gentle simmer (170-180°F — small bubbles, not a rolling boil). Add jumbo shrimp (U-10 or U-12, shell-on). Poach 4-6 minutes until pink and opaque throughout. Do not overcook — shrimp should curl into a loose C shape, not a tight O. |
| Ice bath | Immediately transfer shrimp to a full ice bath. Cool completely (5 minutes). |
| Peel & devein | Peel shells, leaving the tail segment intact (for guests to hold). Devein with a paring knife. |
| Store | Arrange shrimp on a parchment-lined sheet pan in a single layer. Cover with damp paper towel, then plastic wrap. Refrigerate until service. |
| Portion cocktail sauce | Fill ramekins (2 oz each) with **PR-5: Cocktail Sauce**. Cover and refrigerate. |

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fill a martini glass or coupe with crushed ice, mounded slightly above the rim. |
| 2 | Nestle one ramekin of **PR-5: Cocktail Sauce** into the center of the ice. |
| 3 | Hang **5 chilled shrimp** around the rim of the glass, tails pointing up, evenly spaced. Shrimp should be curved over the rim so they're easy to grab. |
| 4 | Place a **lemon wedge** on the rim between two shrimp. |
| 5 | Set glass on an underliner plate. Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Cold plating — 1 minute build time.
- Shrimp must be cold. If they've been sitting out, re-chill before plating.

### APP-2: Iceberg Wedge Salad

**Station:** Garde Manger (Cold)

#### Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Cook bacon | Dice applewood-smoked thick-cut bacon into 1/4-inch pieces. Render in a skillet over medium heat until crispy (8-10 minutes). Drain on paper towels. Store in a deli container at room temperature for service. |
| Prep tomatoes | Wash grape tomatoes. Halve lengthwise. Store in a deli container, refrigerated. |
| Portion dressing | Ensure **PR-6: Blue Cheese Dressing** is in a squeeze bottle or ladle-ready container. |
| Prep blue cheese crumbles | Portion blue cheese crumbles (1 tbsp per plate) into a mise container. |

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Select a firm, tight head of iceberg lettuce. Cut into quarters through the core (the core holds the wedge together). Trim any wilted or browned outer leaves. |
| 2 | Place the **wedge upright** (cut-side facing out) on a chilled salad plate. |
| 3 | Ladle or squeeze **2-3 tbsp PR-6: Blue Cheese Dressing** generously over the top of the wedge, letting it cascade down the sides. |
| 4 | Scatter **1 tbsp blue cheese crumbles** over the dressing. |
| 5 | Scatter **1 tbsp crispy bacon bits** over the top. |
| 6 | Arrange **5-6 halved grape tomatoes** around the base of the wedge. |
| 7 | Finish with **fresh cracked black pepper** (3-4 turns of the mill). |
| 8 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Cold plating — 2 minute build time.
- Cut the wedge to order. Pre-cut wedges oxidize and brown on the edges.

### APP-3: Seared Steak Bites

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Portion tenderloin | Cut beef tenderloin trim or tail pieces into 1-inch cubes. You need 5-6 cubes per order (~4 oz). Portion into individual containers. Refrigerate. |
| Stage seasoning | Ensure **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning** shaker is stocked at the station. |
| Stage butter | Ensure **PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** log is sliced into medallions and held in a cold mise container at the station. |

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pull one portion of tenderloin cubes. Pat dry with a paper towel (dry surface = better sear). |
| 2 | Season all sides generously with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning**. |
| 3 | Heat a cast iron skillet over **high heat** until smoking. Add 1 tbsp canola oil (high smoke point). |
| 4 | Place cubes in the skillet in a single layer — **do not crowd the pan**. If the pan is too small, work in batches. |
| 5 | Sear undisturbed 45 seconds per side, turning with tongs, until a brown crust forms on all sides. Total cook time ~2.5-3 minutes for **medium-rare** (warm red center). |
| 6 | Kill the heat. Drop **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** into the skillet on top of the steak bites. |
| 7 | Transfer the bites and melting butter to a **pre-heated sizzle plate** (or small cast iron). The butter should be visibly melting and sizzling. |
| 8 | Insert 5-6 toothpicks into the bites for easy grabbing. |
| 9 | Set sizzle plate on an underliner plate (protect the table from heat). Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Hot app — 4 minute total cook time.
- Fire when the ticket comes in. This does not hold — serve immediately off the pan.
- **IMPORTANT:** Warn the guest — "Careful, the plate is very hot."

## ENTREES

> **All 5 steaks follow the same core method.** The only differences are the cut, weight, and cook time.
> All are seasoned with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning** and finished with **PR-2: Herb Compound Butter**.

---

### General Steak Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Temper steaks | Pull all steaks from walk-in refrigerator. Place on sheet pans, single layer, covered loosely with plastic. Let come to room temperature for **30-45 minutes** before service begins. Cold steaks cook unevenly (charred outside, raw center). |
| Stage seasoning | Fill and position **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning** shaker at the grill station. |
| Stage butter | Slice **PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** into medallions. Hold in a covered mise container on ice at the station. |
| Heat broiler/grill | Preheat broiler to maximum or grill grates to 500°F+ at least 20 minutes before first ticket. |

---

### Steak Temperature Guide

| Doneness | Target Finish Temp | Pull Temp (remove from heat) | Center Color |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rare | 125°F | 120°F | Cool red |
| Medium-Rare | 130-135°F | 125°F | Warm red |
| Medium | 140°F | 135°F | Warm pink |
| Medium-Well | 150°F | 145°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F | 155°F | No pink |

> **Always pull 5°F below the target finish temp.** Steaks continue cooking during the rest (carry-over heat raises temp 5-8°F). Use an instant-read thermometer — do not guess.

### ENT-1: 14 oz Bone-In Ribeye

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pat steak dry with paper towel. Season **all sides** generously with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning** — top, bottom, edges, and around the bone. |
| 2 | Place on the hottest part of the grill or under the broiler, **presentation-side down first** (the side that will face the guest gets the best grill marks). |
| 3 | Sear/broil 5-6 minutes on the first side without moving. Flip once. |
| 4 | Continue cooking 4-6 minutes on the second side, checking internal temp with an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part (away from the bone). Pull at **5°F below target**. |
| 5 | **Rest:** Transfer to a warm plate or cutting board. Rest **5 full minutes** — resting redistributes juices. Cutting early = juices on the board, not in the steak. |
| 6 | Place rested steak on a **heated dinner plate** (plates should be warming in the pass). Position the bone facing away from the guest. |
| 7 | Place **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** directly on top of the steak. It should begin melting immediately on the hot surface. |
| 8 | Serve immediately. Sides are plated separately (see Side specs). |

#### Timing

- Fire to pass: **12-16 minutes** (including rest) for medium-rare.
- Bone-in cuts take longer than boneless — bone insulates the meat. Err on checking temp early.

### ENT-2: 8 oz Filet Mignon

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pat steak dry. Season **all sides** with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning**. Because the filet is thick and cylindrical, make sure to season the edges/barrel, not just the flat faces. |
| 2 | Place on grill or under broiler, flat-side down. |
| 3 | Sear 4-5 minutes on the first side. Flip. |
| 4 | Sear 4-5 minutes on the second side. For thick filets (2+ inches), **also sear the edges** by holding the steak on its side with tongs for 1-2 minutes. |
| 5 | Check internal temp. Pull at 5°F below target. |
| 6 | **Rest 5 minutes** on a warm plate. |
| 7 | Place on a **heated dinner plate**. |
| 8 | Place **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** on top. |
| 9 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Fire to pass: **12-15 minutes** (including rest) for medium-rare.
- Filets are the most commonly ordered medium-rare to medium. Overcooking a filet is costly — check temp early and often.
- The filet is lean with no fat cap — the compound butter is critical for richness. Do not skip it.

### ENT-3: 12 oz New York Strip

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pat steak dry. Season **all sides** with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning**. Season the fat cap edge as well. |
| 2 | Place on grill or under broiler, **fat-cap edge down first** for 1-2 minutes to render and crisp the fat. Then lay flat, presentation-side down. |
| 3 | Sear 4-5 minutes. Flip once. |
| 4 | Continue cooking 3-5 minutes on the second side. Check internal temp. Pull at 5°F below target. |
| 5 | **Rest 5 minutes.** |
| 6 | Place on a **heated dinner plate**. Position with fat cap facing away from the guest. |
| 7 | Place **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** on top. |
| 8 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Fire to pass: **11-14 minutes** (including rest) for medium-rare.
- The fat cap on a strip should be golden and rendered, not white and rubbery. Searing the edge first achieves this.

### ENT-4: 20 oz Bone-In Cowboy Cut

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pat steak dry. This is a large, thick cut. Season **very generously** on all sides with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning** — more surface area demands more seasoning. Season around the bone. |
| 2 | Place on grill or under broiler, presentation-side down. |
| 3 | Sear 6-7 minutes on the first side. Flip. |
| 4 | Continue cooking 5-7 minutes on the second side. Because of the thickness (often 2+ inches), this cut may need to be **moved to a cooler zone** of the grill or **finished in a 400°F oven** for 4-6 minutes to bring the center to temp without burning the crust. |
| 5 | Check internal temp in the thickest part, away from the bone. Pull at 5°F below target. |
| 6 | **Rest 10 minutes** — longer rest than other steaks due to the larger mass. Do not cut short. |
| 7 | Place on a **heated dinner plate**. Position the bone as a visual feature (e.g., pointing up or to the side). |
| 8 | Place **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** on top. |
| 9 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Fire to pass: **20-24 minutes** (including 10-min rest) for medium-rare.
- This is the longest cook on the menu. **Fire this first** if the table has multiple entrees. Coordinate with expo.
- Thick bone-in cuts benefit from the reverse approach: lower heat to bring up internal temp, then a final blast of high heat for crust. If your broiler has zones, use them.

### ENT-5: 6 oz Petite Filet

**Station:** Grill / Broiler

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pat steak dry. Season **all sides** with **PR-1: House Steak Seasoning**. |
| 2 | Place on grill or under broiler, flat-side down. |
| 3 | Sear 3-4 minutes on the first side. Flip. |
| 4 | Sear 3-4 minutes on the second side. Check internal temp. Pull at 5°F below target. |
| 5 | **Rest 4 minutes.** |
| 6 | Place on a **heated dinner plate**. |
| 7 | Place **1 medallion PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** on top. |
| 8 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- Fire to pass: **10-12 minutes** (including rest) for medium-rare.
- Smallest and fastest steak on the menu. If a table orders this alongside a Cowboy Cut, fire the Cowboy Cut first and stagger the Petite Filet.
- Same lean-cut note as the 8 oz Filet — compound butter is essential for richness.

## SIDES

> Sides are plated in individual side dishes (not on the steak plate).
> Both sides are batch-prepped and held warm. The line cook portions to order.

---

### SIDE-1: Truffle Mashed Potatoes

**Station:** Hot Line

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Stir the hotel pan of **PR-3: Truffle Mashed Potatoes** before portioning (settling can occur during holding). |
| 2 | Spoon a **6 oz portion** into a warm side dish. Use a kitchen scale until you can eyeball it consistently. |
| 3 | Smooth the top with the back of the spoon and create a shallow well in the center. |
| 4 | *(Optional garnish)* Place a small piece of **PR-2: Herb Compound Butter** in the well, or add a tiny drizzle of truffle oil. Fresh chive snips also work. |
| 5 | Place on the pass. |

#### Timing

- 30 seconds to plate. This is a scoop-and-go side.
- If the mash has thickened during holding, stir in a small splash of warm cream before portioning.

---

### SIDE-2: Creamed Spinach

**Station:** Hot Line

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Stir the hotel pan of **PR-4: Creamed Spinach** before portioning. |
| 2 | Spoon a **5 oz portion** into a warm side dish. |
| 3 | *(Optional garnish)* Light grating of fresh nutmeg on top. |
| 4 | Place on the pass. |

- If the spinach has thickened or tightened during holding, stir in a small splash of warm cream.

## DESSERTS

### DES-1: New York Cheesecake

**Station:** Dessert

#### Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Stage cheesecake | Ensure **PR-8: New York Cheesecake** is pre-sliced (12 slices) and refrigerated. |
| Stage compote | Ensure **PR-7: Berry Compote** is in a squeeze bottle or small ladle-ready saucepan. If serving warm, heat gently at the start of service. |
| Prep mint | Pick fresh mint leaves, wash, pat dry. Store in a damp paper towel in a small container. |

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Using a thin offset spatula, carefully transfer **1 pre-cut slice of PR-8: New York Cheesecake** to the center of a room-temp dessert plate. Handle gently — the slice should arrive intact with clean edges. |
| 2 | Spoon or squeeze **2-3 tbsp PR-7: Berry Compote** over the top of the slice, allowing it to cascade down one side and pool slightly on the plate. |
| 3 | Place a **fresh mint sprig** on top of the compote, leaning against the cheesecake. |
| 4 | Serve immediately. |

#### Timing

- 1 minute to plate. Cold dessert — no cooking required.
- Cheesecake is served **cold**. The warm or room-temp Berry Compote provides a temperature contrast.

### DES-2: Warm Brownie à la Mode

**Station:** Dessert

#### Station Prep ★ (beginning of shift)

| Task | Method |
|---|---|
| Stage brownies | Ensure pre-cut portions from **PR-9: Brownie Batch** are in a covered container at the station, ready to warm. |
| Stage compote | Ensure **PR-7: Berry Compote** is in a squeeze bottle or small ladle-ready saucepan. Same as cheesecake station prep. |
| Stage ice cream | Ensure vanilla bean ice cream is in the freezer with a scoop and hot water bath for the scoop. |

#### Build (to order)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Warm the brownie:** Place 1 portion of **PR-9: Brownie Batch** on a microwave-safe plate. Microwave 15-20 seconds until warm throughout but not hot. *(Alternatively: warm in a 350°F oven for 2-3 minutes.)* The brownie should be soft and slightly gooey, not stiff or dried out. |
| 2 | Transfer the warm brownie to the center of a room-temp dessert plate. |
| 3 | Place **1 scoop vanilla bean ice cream** on top of the brownie, slightly off-center. Use a dipped-in-hot-water scoop for a clean, round ball. |
| 4 | Spoon or squeeze **2-3 tbsp PR-7: Berry Compote** over the ice cream, letting it drip down the sides of the brownie and onto the plate. |
| 5 | *(Optional garnish)* Light dusting of cocoa powder on the plate rim using a fine-mesh sieve. |
| 6 | **Serve immediately.** Ice cream is melting — this plate waits for no one. |

#### Timing

- 2 minutes to plate (including warm time).
- **Fire when the expo calls it.** Do not warm the brownie early — it will dry out.
- This is a race against the ice cream. Plate and send. No delays.
