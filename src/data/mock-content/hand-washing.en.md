# Hand Washing Protocol

Proper hand washing is the **single most important** practice to prevent the spread of foodborne illness. All team members must follow this protocol without exception.

> **‚ö†Ô∏è Critical**: Wash hands for a minimum of 20 seconds using the technique described below.

## When to Wash Hands

You **must** wash your hands:

- Before starting work
- Before putting on gloves
- After using the restroom
- After touching your face, hair, or body
- After sneezing, coughing, or blowing your nose
- After handling raw meat, poultry, or seafood
- After handling garbage or cleaning chemicals
- After touching dirty dishes or equipment
- After eating, drinking, or smoking
- After handling money
- When switching between tasks

> **üí° Tip**: When in doubt, wash your hands. It's better to wash too often than not enough.

## The 6-Step Hand Washing Technique

Follow these steps every time you wash your hands:

### Step 1: Wet

Wet hands with warm running water (at least 100¬∞F / 38¬∞C).

### Step 2: Apply Soap

Apply enough soap to cover all hand surfaces.

### Step 3: Lather

Lather by rubbing hands together, ensuring you clean:

- Palms
- Back of hands
- Between fingers
- Under fingernails
- Wrists

### Step 4: Scrub

Scrub hands for at least **20 seconds**. 

> **‚ÑπÔ∏è Note**: Sing "Happy Birthday" twice, or count slowly to 20.

### Step 5: Rinse

Rinse thoroughly under clean, running water.

### Step 6: Dry

Dry hands completely using:

- Single-use paper towels (preferred)
- Air dryer
- Clean cloth towel (changed frequently)

> **‚ö†Ô∏è Critical**: Never wash hands in prep sinks, dish sinks, or mop sinks. Use **designated hand sinks only**.

## Hand Sanitizer

Hand sanitizer is **not a substitute** for hand washing. It may only be used:

- As an additional step after proper hand washing
- When hands are not visibly soiled
- Between glove changes (in addition to washing)

Sanitizer must contain at least **60% alcohol** to be effective.

## Glove Usage

Gloves do not replace hand washing. You must:

1. Wash hands before putting on gloves
2. Change gloves when switching tasks
3. Change gloves after touching non-food surfaces
4. Never reuse disposable gloves
5. Wash hands after removing gloves
